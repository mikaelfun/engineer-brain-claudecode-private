# Every attempted contact means:

- You tried to contact the customer or partner by using both email and telephone. The exception is when the customer or partner specifically asks to use email only.
- First contact made to customer after accepting a case, does not count as Follow-up 1 (Follow up)

# Unresponsive customer means:

1. The customer failed to respond to the contact notification email.
2. If the phone number and/or email address on the case's contact is invalid, all due diligence should be done to correct the situation. For instance contact the IM+ or search on previous cases for the right contact email or phone number

**If the customer or partner responds at any point in the workflow:**
- Schedule the call for this case at your earliest convenience and preferable within the time frame indicated by the customer, if any.
- If they become unresponsive again in future the follow up count will restart at 1.
- If action is on Customer to provide data or follow up on an action plan the follow up counter continues until Customer is able to provide required data or hit a road block.

### **Every attempt to contact the customer must be documented in the case notes, along with the next intended contact date and time.
### Please make time adjustments for Weekends and Holidays as the customer may not be able to work on the case in these days.**


# follow up procedure workflow

## Follow up 1 - no response within 1 business day to previous contact attempt
1. Contact attempted by telephone with no contact (leave voicemail, if possible).
1. Send email message to get feedback from customer
1. Set the case status to - Waiting on Customer (DFM):


## Follow up 2 - no response within 1 business day to previous contact attempt

1. Contact attempted by telephone with no contact (leave voicemail, if possible).
1. Send email message to get feedback from customer. Tell the customer when you will close the case if there is no further response.  A deadline often motivates customers to respond.
1. Check case status - Waiting on Customer (DFM)- still must be on DFM: Waiting on Customer
1. Set follow up count to Follow up 2 (when feature available on case Notes)
1. ***For Premier customers and partners, email the IM+ (and Incident Manager if known) directly, explaining the current delays, any reason you're aware of for the delays, the timelines for us closing the case and clearly stating your request for the IM+ to engage with the customer to help drive case progression.

## Follow up 3 - no response within 1 business day to previous contact attempt

1. Contact attempted by telephone with no contact (leave voicemail, if possible).
1. Email message sent (using the Closure - Unresponsive Customer follow up template) closing the case.
1. Set Case Status - Waiting on Customer DFM Status: Waiting on Customer
1. Set follow up count to Follow up 3 (when feature available in DFM)
1. Ping the IM+ on teams and confirm if he have another contact to reach the customer or if he can try to reach the customer. Inform the IM+  of all your contact attempts and confirm if we can close the case.
1. In case you can't get in contact with the IM+ or customer, close the case using the following, even if a solution was offered: DFM Resolution: Unresolved

NOTE:
- It is important that contact attempts only take place during the customer's workday.  Calling a customer when they are likely to be at lunch or you believe they are gone for the day doesn't count towards 3X.  Check the customer's time zone and look at their signature for hints of best contact times.
- Different days and times are important.  If the customer attends a 2pm meeting everyday then calling 3 times during that meeting will only frustrate the customer.
- If the customer does not consider phone acceptable then simply use email and/or the Web communication option.
- Go the extra distance and check for holidays in the customer's country/region.
- It is critical we never come across as blaming the customer for not being there.  They are no more psychic than we are and don't know when you might call.
- Tell the customer when you will close the case if there is no further response.  A deadline often motivates customers to respond.
- If you are speaking with the customer, the case is at Zero X.  If you make a follow up contact attempt update the case title to 1X.  If the customer replies delete the 1X from the case title.  Progress to 2X and 3X in similar fashion.  Close the case 1 business day after making the 3X contact attempt. Check this - Make sure that both TAs and the manager are in cc before the case closure.
