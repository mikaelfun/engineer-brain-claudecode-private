# Overview
Misroutes are one of the main drivers of delayed support for our customers. So, we must be certain that a case was misrouted before attempting to transfer it to another queue.

If the destined queue is also incorrect, it could lead to the case "bouncing" between queues and cause delays for our customer. This behavior can negatively impact the overall support experience our customers have.
We must then take actions to minimize or fully prevent any cases "bouncing" between the same queues, and that any transfers are done without causing as little delay as possible to our customers.

## Possible Causes
- The customer selected the wrong Support Area Path (SAP) or affected resources during case creation.
- Manual transfer of a case to a queue, instead of using auto-routing by updating the SAP.
- Support engineer creates a task without confirming the SAP.
- Rejection of transfer due to SLA status (expired or missed) or due to high case age.
