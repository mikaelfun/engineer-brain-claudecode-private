There are occasions when real-time or in-time collaboration is required to resolve Customer Support Cases.
This collaboration policy helps to ensure that members of the CSS Organization are efficiently working together to resolve Support Cases in a timely manner, always placing customers at the center of everything they do.

**Best Practices:**

It's important for all Support Engineers to be accountable, resourceful and show empathy, communicating efficiently throughout the entire case lifecycle.

If there is an escalation, at any point throughout the case lifecycle, make sure all the Partner Technical Advisors (PTA) of the related technologies are aware and involved. PTAs can look into the issue and action plan to decide on the next steps and if they will stay involved, however, both the Case and Collaboration Task Owners are responsible for bringing it to PTAs attention.

When identifying the need to work together with another Microsoft Support Team, it's under the Case Owner's responsibility to:

Always write a clear and specific collaboration Task description about the issue and what is needed from the collaboration Task's Target Team.

As soon as the collaboration Task gets assigned, the Case Owner will be approaching the Collaborator (Task Owner) directly via MS Teams to discuss about the issue, ensure there is a common understanding of the issue and end goal, and agree on the next steps/ action plan, together.

Lead the Communication with the Customer and all the collaborators who're engaged. No matter who the next step for the case progression depends on – if there is an active collaboration with a different Support Team/ a SME/ a PTA/ a member of the Product Group, etc. – the Case Owner has the Lead Role in the smooth progression of the case.

Create Swarming channels: Always create an internal Teams group chat with all those involved in the case (Collaborators, PTAs) to communicate and ensure there is consistent progress towards case resolution.

Even if the action is pending on a collaborator, the recommended approach is for the Case Owner to schedule and join all of the calls with the Customer and the Collaborator(s).

Document everything on case notes: from the issue description, customer goal, troubleshooting steps taken and their results, checks performed and their results, what has been discussed with the Collaborators and with the Customer via Chat/ Phone/ Email, what the next actions and next contact date are, to any other relevant details.

**Note**: If unable to attend a Customer call with the Collaborator, the Case Owner needs to follow up internally with the Collaborator to understand the actions are taken and current status, rather than following up with the customer.

What is expected from all swarming channels?

Full collaborative approach to move the cases forward.
Delivering a good customer experience by keeping in mind the best interest of the customer.
Good communication flow between all parties involved
What is not expected from all swarming channels:

Push back on collaborations or case transfers.
Accountability disputes.
Ignore help requests or disengage before case progression is assured.
This is the OneMicrosoft seamless approach to helping, advising and empowering Customers.
