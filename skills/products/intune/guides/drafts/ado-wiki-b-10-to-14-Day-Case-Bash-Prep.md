# Presenting Your Case at the 10–14 Day Case Bash

The 10-14 Day Case Bash is designed to help case owners move their case closer to resolution faster. Since many cases are presented during these triages, it is critical to come prepared.

## Be Prepared

- Is your case correctly managed and documented?
- The **Customer Information Template** should be filled out as much as possible but absolutely should have a scope defined and agreed upon by the customer.
- The template should be **pinned in the Summary tab**.
- Having the relevant DeviceID, UserID, UPN, etc. readily available (should be in the template) is very important.
- If asked, can you speak to the results of guidance in the Intune wiki related to your topic?
- Review the wiki for your topic to help anticipate questions that may be asked in the triage.
- **Pin relevant case notes** in the Timeline or Summary tab.
- Don't waste time scrolling through your case trying to find the note with log analysis, screenshots, etc.

## Be Concise

- Don't go through every single step that has occurred in the case.
- In a few sentences, and within **30-60 seconds**, introduce your case with:
  - The **scope** of the case (a good scope makes the issue and end goal clear)
  - **Current state**, which may include one or more of:
    - Brief statement on last action plan and results
    - Brief statement on data collected and analysis
    - Has a repro been done, yes or no, and were results the same, yes or no?
    - Research conducted
    - Who has the next action in the case
- If you have a specific ask (e.g., "How do I review these logs?"), be sure to ask it.
- Mention any roadblocks.

## Actively Listen

- Active listening to the guidance you receive is important.
- **Document in the case** the guidance received and next steps.

## What to Do if You Cannot Attend

- If you're unable to attend due to a CritSit, unexpected time off, or another unforeseen circumstance, **notify your TA as soon as possible — no later than one hour before**.
- If they're available, your TA can present your case on your behalf.
- To enable your TA to present accurately:
  - Make sure your case notes are up to date.
  - Share any questions you'd like addressed so your TA can raise them for you.
- It's **recommended to avoid scheduling customer calls during Case Bash** unless absolutely necessary.
