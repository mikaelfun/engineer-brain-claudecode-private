**Duplicate Case Overview**

There are times in which customers may open duplicate cases. When this happens there are several steps to take once it is determined that there are duplicate cases.

**What is a Duplicate Case?**

A duplicate case is a second/additional case that is raised for the same specific issue. E.g. Customer opens individual cases for two users who are having issues accessing a resource - when the reason for these user access issues is the same. This would be considered duplicate.

_**If you have any doubts regarding if a case is a duplicate please reach out to your PTA**_


**Duplicate Cases Process**

1. When a duplicate case is discovered, the engineers owning both cases should communicate with each other to determine which case is further along in the troubleshooting process.
2. Once decided, the engineers should inform the customer that they will be closing one case as duplicate in order to avoid duplication of work and efforts.
3. After informing the customer, the engineer that will be closing their case should close the case as duplicate in DFM and communicate with the customer as below:

<Hi or Hello optional> <customer's first name for most countries/cultures>
I'm reaching out about your issue related to <summary of issue description>
It appears you have two cases with the same issue. To avoid duplication, I've closed case # <case being closed>. Going forward, please use case # <case staying open>.
Thanks,

<insert signature>
