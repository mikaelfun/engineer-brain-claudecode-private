# All Hands on Deck (AHOD) Rules

**Page Author:** Rashid Siddiqui

## 1. Case Ownership Expectations  

- Every technical professional, regardless of role or designation, may be required to take cases.  
- Managers who are technically trained may be asked to actively work customer cases.  

## 2. Assignment & Capacity Controls  

- Engineers may be asked to temporarily exceed the standard maximum capacity.  
- SEEs will focus on fresh case intake to stabilize inflow.  
- NonS500 cases may be blocked from assignment during the surge.  
- Broad Commercial cases may be paused for new assignments.  

## 3. Priority Protection  

- SevA / S500 cases take absolute priority.  
- Escalations for SevB and SevC may be delayed to preserve capacity for critical customers.  

## 4. Operational Adjustments  

- T2G activities may be canceled or deferred.  
- OutofOffice (OOF) approvals may be temporarily placed on hold.  

All actions are reviewed continuously and reversed as soon as conditions allow.  

## Why These Measures Are Necessary  

During extreme volume surges, treating all work equally creates systemic risk. AHOD rules are designed to:  

- Protect critical customers first (SevA / S500).  
- Prevent SLA breaches that have long-term trust and business impact.  
- Reduce queue volatility and restore predictable flow.  
- Avoid burnout caused by uncontrolled backlog growth.  
- Stabilize the system quickly, rather than allowing prolonged degradation.
