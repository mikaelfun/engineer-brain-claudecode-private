## Applicable to
- Americas S500 and Prem engineers
- EMEA S500 and Prem engineers

## Unplanned OOF
- Notify your manager.
- Notify the sick line DL so that WFM and VDM are blocked for the day (May vary per region).
- Let your TAs know about any case that requires urgent attention while you are OOF.

## Planned OOF (<=2 days)
- Make sure WFM reflects you being OOF for the required days.
- Let the customers know you will be out of the office for a couple of days.
- If something cannot wait, look for a backup that can monitor the case or meet with the customer while you're out. If you struggle to find a backup, notify your TAs.
- Case ownership should not change for this scenario.
- No collaboration task should be opened for this backup scenario.

## Planned OOF (>2 days)
- Make sure WFM reflects you being OOF for the required days.
- Depending on case volume, you may ask to be blocked in VDM a day or two before your planned OOF.
- Before leaving, send an email that contains a list of your cases to your TAs, brief description of the issue, current status and next actions. Make sure your notes in each one of the cases are up to date as well.
- ARM TAs will look at the backlogs of your peers, case complexity and next planned OOFs to redistribute those cases across the team.
- You are not expected to take any of the cases back when returning. However, there might be special scenarios where doing so might be better for the customer, this is the exception, not the rule.

## FAQ

### How do we ensure there is enough coverage when people ask to be OOF?
Managers will check that the overall people OOF for that day is below 20% of the headcount. 20% of people out should still be manageable.

### What if people just take some days OOF to reset their backlogs?
**Microsoft runs on trust**. If this happens, we don't have a problem with process, we have a more serious problem that needs to be addressed differently. Regardless of that, TAs have processes in place to ensure cases are moving forward and engineers are comfortable working the cases they have assigned. If an engineer has a challenge with their backlog, this should be raised so that we can work on the real causes that led to that situation.

### Why are we not expecting engineers to take back their backlog when they are back?
A few reasons: 

The first one is that acting as backup does not provide the same level of accountability as if the case ownership now is yours. If the case is not expected to be returned, the new case owner is fully responsible of that case being worked and closed with all best practices we know. This is the main reason for this decision.

Second one is related to the customer not being bounced between engineers that may lose context of the last actions performed on the case.

Another factor is that redistributing the backlog is an opportunity to balance the workload across the team. When TAs reassign the cases they will look at current backlogs and make sure everyone ends up with a similar number of cases. Taking back the cases will undo that effort.

And finally, we want to disconnect when we are OOF. When we take days off, in many occasions is because we are burnt out. Having to come back to the same state you left, may burn you out quickly again. This approach means you start fresh when you are back.
