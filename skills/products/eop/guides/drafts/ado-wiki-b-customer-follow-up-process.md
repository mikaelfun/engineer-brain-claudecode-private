The purpose of this process is to provide a structured and consistent approach when dealing with unresponsive customers. These steps have proven to increase the chances of customers responding to follow-ups.

1. Send the first follow-up email 24-48 hours from your last communication with the customer.
2. If no answer after 48 hours, **try calling the customer on the phone from DFM and try to reach them on Teams chat**. If no luck, leave a voice message and send a second follow-up email. Be mindful of the customer time zone and avoid calling outside their working hours.
3. If still no answer after 48 hours, **try calling the customer on the phone from DFM and try to reach them on Teams chat**. If no luck, leave a voice message and send a third follow-up email **informing the cx that we'll have to archive the case due to its inactivity (you can mention the day when you plan to archive the case)** and send an email to the IM/CSAM to ask for help with a follow-up with the customer.
4. If still no answer after 48 hours, ask your TA for a recovery call. A recovery call may not be needed if a solution was already provided to the customer and they confirmed that the issue is fixed. Double-check with your TA if unsure.
5. When calculating the 48 hours, exclude the weekends and be mindful about possible local holidays.
6. If the customer primary contact is out of the office and no backup contact or no return date or that's more than 1-2 days, contact the CSAM/IM and ask for a back-up contact person from the customer side.
7. For engineers working a 4/10 shift, the follow-up timing may differ for specific cases/scenarios. Please check with your TA for additional guidance if needed.

Please review the below email and [Manager Recovery Procedure - Overview](https://dev.azure.com/ASIM-Security/Messaging%20Protection/_wiki/wikis/Messaging%20Protection%20Wiki/8197/Manager-Recovery-Procedure), as a refresher of the Manager Recovery Process. For Unresponsive customers (3X process) the recovery needs to go to your TA. For all the other scenarios in [Manager Recovery Procedure - Overview](https://dev.azure.com/ASIM-Security/Messaging%20Protection/_wiki/wikis/Messaging%20Protection%20Wiki/8197/Manager-Recovery-Procedure), the recovery needs to go to your manager.

**ADDITIONAL NOTE:**
      
There were reports that some customers, especially the ones located in the US are not answering or even blocking international (non-US) phone numbers and this is affecting our follow-up process leading to an increase request for manager recovery for unresponsive customers and an unnecessary delay in closing cases.
Therefore, when doing call follow-ups for unresponsive customers, please call from within DFM instead of using Teams, as this way the call will be done using an US phone number instead of your local phone number and it'll increase the chances of the customers answering your follow-up call.

If you have any issues performing calls from OnceVoice in DFM, please try to follow the steps provided in the Warning or the Error message to fix the issue or raise a support ticket for assistance: [End User Support](https://bts.microsoftcrmportals.com/SignIn?ReturnUrl=%2Fsupport%2Fcreate-case%2F)

Please also note that when calling from DFM, you have the option to transfer the customer to the IVR survey at the end of your call, should the customer agree. While you are NOT allowed to ask for a 5-stars or a good survey, you can ask customers if they would like to provide feedback about your work or/and for the support received.

The primary goal of these calls is to confirm customer satisfaction with both the **product** and **support experience**. IVR is not solely a survey tool - it is an opportunity to build rapport and ensure the customer feels genuinely supported and leave the case feeling confident that Microsoft cares.  

### During the Call

*   **Greet warmly:** Be cordial and ask how they're doing,
*   **Check in on resolution:** Confirm that their issue has been fully resolved,
*   **Probe for outstanding needs:** Ask if they are experiencing any other issues you may be able to help with or guide them on,
*   **Close positively:** Leave the customer feeling heard and valued.
