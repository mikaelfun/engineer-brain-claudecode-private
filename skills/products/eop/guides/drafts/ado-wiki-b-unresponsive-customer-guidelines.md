#Unresponsive customer guidelines: Re-establishing contact with the customer

[[_TOC_]]

##Objective
These guidelines are designed to help support engineers manage cases where customers become unresponsive.

The goal is to re-engage the customer and progress the support case effectively. This should follow the timelines defined in the official DDX procedure [Procedure: Unresponsive Customer](https://internal.evergreen.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/96fac429-4ec2-47d7-deff-7bf3e35ffffe#bkmk_3).


##Process
In the [Procedure: Unresponsive Customer](https://internal.evergreen.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/96fac429-4ec2-47d7-deff-7bf3e35ffffe#bkmk_3), the following steps apply:

###What does "everything" entail and what can I do to re-establish contact with the customer?
1.  Identify Alternative Contacts:
    *   Ask the Incident Manager (IM+) for additional customer contacts if they are not available in the case details. If the IM+ is unresponsive:
        *   Check the current IM+ out-of-office (OOO) message for additional contacts.
        *   Look for additional IM+ contacts in the case details.
        *   Email the 24x7casemanagement@microsoft.com alias for additional IM+ contacts. [This team can provide an IM+ for any case]
        *   Seek assistance from the Customer Success Account Manager (CSAM) for additional contacts from the customer or another IM+.
        *   If still unable to get a response, reach out to the original IM's manager to assign another IM+ to the case.

2.  Check Customer's Email Signature or OOO Message:
    *   Look for backup contacts or other contact methods in the customer's email signature or OOO message.

3.  Use Multiple Communication Channels:
    *   Use both phone and email to contact the customer, unless they have specified a preference for email only.

4.  Inform CSAM if Case is Pending PG's DCR/Bug Fix:
    *   If the case is pending a Product Group's Design Change Request (DCR) or bug fix when the customer becomes unresponsive, inform the CSAM and involve them in the discussion with the customer.

5. **IMPORTANT**: Create a Recovery Request (MUST DO for any unresponsive customer):
    *   After exhausting all methods to contact the customer, create a recovery request as per the current process.

6.  If the customer is on vacation, follow the procedure outlined in the official DDX [Procedure: Customer out of office (OOF)](https://internal.evergreen.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/6d53142a-b118-737d-bbc0-6fe8e16f325b).

##Important Notes
*   **IMPORTANT: Avoid using the "three strikes" rule** due to its negative connotations.
*   Maintain ownership of the case and continue working on it as needed.
*   Document all communications in the case notes.
