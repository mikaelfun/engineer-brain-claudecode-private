# CPE Scenario 7 \u2013 The customer doesn\u2019t agree with your troubleshooting approach

## Scenario Details

- You provide a certain action plan to the customer, but the customer doesn\u2019t want to implement it, questioning if it\u2019s relevant to the issue or not.

## Risky Actions That Might Lead to Poor CPE Results

**Possible Action:**
- You mention that the same action plan has resolved the same issue in a few other cases of yours.

**Possible Result:**
- Some customers don\u2019t accept such general justifications and may think that you just provide a generic action plan that could be found here and there. As a result, they may not want to follow your action plan.

## Recommendations to Achieve Better CPE Results

- Ideally, you should be able to provide enough **technical justifications** along with the action plan items so that customers could be better persuaded to implement the action plan.
- Generally, the best justifications are the **technical ones backed with your findings from the logs** that were previously collected by the customer.
- That also shows the value of proper data collection to the customer, and they will be eager to collect new ones if and when you ask them to do so.
