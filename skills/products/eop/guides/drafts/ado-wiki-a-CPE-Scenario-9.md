# CPE Scenario 9 \u2013 Advising the customer to use or not to use a certain product

## Scenario Details

- You help the customer repair a corrupted Windows Update database by temporarily removing the customer\u2019s 3rd party antivirus software. When the customer asks why the database was corrupted, this scenario covers how to handle product recommendations.

## Risky Actions That Might Lead to Poor CPE Results

**Possible Action:**
- You ask the customer to stop using a piece of 3rd party antivirus software and use Windows Defender instead, mentioning that some 3rd party antivirus software may show false positives or lead to performance problems.

**Possible Result:**
- The customer will possibly have an internal discussion with their IT security team and there might be some internal conflict in the end.

## Recommendations to Achieve Better CPE Results

### Actions That Are Fine to Take

- Suggest the customer to **temporarily** uninstall or disable a piece of 3rd party software to troubleshoot the current problem.
- Recommend the customer to discuss with the 3rd party software/hardware support vendor to troubleshoot the issue.

### Actions That Should Always Be Avoided

- **Do not** tell customers to permanently stop using a piece of 3rd party software.
- Customers have the freedom to choose their own software solutions. A support engineer should **not** try to influence customers to pick Microsoft\u2019s solutions.
- Do **not** share opinions or make comments on how well or poorly a piece of 3rd party software works.
