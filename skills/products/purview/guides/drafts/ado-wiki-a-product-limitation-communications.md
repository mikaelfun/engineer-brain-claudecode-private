**How to handle/communicate Product Limitation scenarios:**

[DDX Official Guidance](https://internal.evergreen.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/91cd643b-038a-ebec-a066-0167a588f737#bkmk_product_limitation)

Even though our PGs strive for perfection, there will be situations where the answer to a case will be related with a product limitation.

The general feeling about communicating these limitations it's typically of hesitance, possibly related with the assumption that a low CSAT might follow. However, regardless if that assumption it's true or not, I'm certain that if we all put ourselves on the customer shoes, we would prefer to receive that information as soon as possible, since the longer it takes, the bigger will the customer frustration be and the lower the trust on us of being able to quickly identify an issue, especially a product limitation.

**Therefore, the request is for you to please do the following on Data Movement Product Limitation scenarios:**

1. Discuss the situation with your SEE, TA and EEE, through the normal channels.

2. Once your SEE, TA and EEE have excluded any possible existence of solution, engage with Product Group.

3. Upon confirmation from a PG Engineer that the issue is related with a product limitation, communicate this immediately to the customer

    For your reference, feel free to reuse the following templates:

    - **DDX:** [Procedure: Case Closure – Last Quality Response (LQR) (DDX Capability)](https://internal.evergreen.microsoft.com/en-us/topic/91cd643b-038a-ebec-a066-0167a588f737#Product_limitation)

    - **ADF wiki**: [Last Quality Response (LQR) - Overview](https://supportability.visualstudio.com/AzureDataFactory/_wiki/wikis/AzureDataFactory/289445/Last-Quality-Response-(LQR)?anchor=by-design-(product-limitation)-email)

4. Regarding the ETA for a bug fix, if we can fix it, please try to get a tentative ETA from the PG Engineering Managers and include it on the LQR email, so that the customer has some timelines. However, if we could not fix it, please share the reason.

5. Should it be a Purview feature request, please direct the customer to the Purview User Voice website:

    - **Purview:** [azure-purview - Microsoft Q&A](https://docs.microsoft.com/en-us/answers/topics/azure-purview.html)

6. Finally, if you have any possible workaround that you can share with the customer to unblock them, please do, however be sure to refer it's nature as not being a permanent solution. If not, proceed with case closure, once you get customer consent.

Please let us (adfeee@microsoft.com) know in case of any doubts.
