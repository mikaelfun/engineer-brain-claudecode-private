# Help me help you - quick guide to requesting help

## TL;DR

1. What is the cx trying to do?
2. What issue are they experiencing?
3. What have you done so far?
4. What is blocking you? What is your specific question?

## When reaching out for help:

### Be prepared
What's the context? What is the cx trying to do? What have you already investigated? What are the specific points you need help with?

### Be proactive
Give a clear picture of the case so that your colleague can understand the issue and context. Don't throw errors or screenshots without context.

### People will be more willing to help you if:
- they see you made an effort to troubleshoot and understand the problem
- they see you learn from previous interactions

### People will be less willing to help you if:
- they see you don't investigate but just want a quick answer
- you're not trying to understand the problem for the future

| DO's | DON'Ts |
|--|--|
| Be prepared | Ask to jump on a call without notice |
| Be specific | Send errors/screenshots without context |
| Be proactive | Be vague about the case |
| Focus on key points of investigation | |
| Clearly identify blockers and raise direct questions | |
| Repro or explain why not possible | |
| Summarize the case | Share raw screenshots of conversations |
