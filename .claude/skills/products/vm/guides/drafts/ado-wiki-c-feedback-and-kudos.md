# Wiki Feedback and Kudos Process

## Summary

This page outlines how to provide feedback on AzureIaaSVM wiki and best practices for providing quality feedback and kudos on wiki content.

## How to Provide Feedback or Kudos on a Wiki Article

Every wiki article owned by a SME team contains a template at the end with an option to provide feedback or kudos. Scroll to the bottom of the page to find the hyperlinks.

The work item template requires:
- Name (user)
- Alias
- User Role (SME, Support Engineer, TA, etc.)
- New or Existing Page?
- Wiki Page Link
- Suggested Changes or New Content Idea (be as detailed as possible) **OR** Kudos
- Supporting Documentation

Once done, press the "Save" icon in the top right corner to save the work item. It will be populated on a SME dashboard for review.

## Feedback and Kudos Best Practices

| Best Practice | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Reference where in the text you're referring to | "Under the section titled X, example 3 is confusing because..." |
| New content should reference all knowledge sources | Include AVA requests, ICM links, public docs links |
| Suggest related tools/references | "This article mentions X, but there's no mention of how to query that. Please add link to..." |
| Ensure no PII in feedback AND wiki article | "The image in section 2, line 42 contains a user alias which is PII. Please edit to remove." |
| Removal of outdated information | Note what is outdated and what the current correct information is |
| Kudos: be specific about what stood out | "The troubleshooting workflow helped me drive a case to resolution. All steps were clear and concise." |
| Easy to read format | Clear, concise sentences; outline, coloring, and bold to emphasize important info |
| Descriptive Work Item titles | Clear, concise summary of the feedback/kudos provided |

## High Quality Feedback

High quality feedback:
- Is specific about which section is confusing
- Provides clear, actionable items for the reviewer to change
- Increases the likelihood of seeing positive change in the article

## Low Quality Feedback

Low quality feedback:
- States that they "couldn't follow" the article without specifying where they got stuck
- Doesn't mention the exact error, what step failed, or troubleshooting steps tried
- Leaves SMEs and Feedback reviewers confused

## How to Check Status of Feedback

Visit the work item created and check the "State" and "Discussion" section to see status and any information from SMEs.

## How to Identify a Content Owner

1. **DO NOT** contact the engineer whose name is listed under the page title.
2. Navigate to the top right corner of the page → select three vertical dots.
3. Select **View revisions** to see the page's full update history.
4. Show all history to identify the original author and who has updated it since.
