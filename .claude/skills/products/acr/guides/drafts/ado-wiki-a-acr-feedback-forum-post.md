# Define a great idea to post on ACR feedback forum

[[_TOC_]]

## Introduction

From time to time we face the opportunity to advise our customers to share in the [Microsoft Azure Feedback Forum](https://aka.ms/ACTInbox/) an idea about something they would like to exist on Azure.

The goal of this article is to share best practices for posting high-quality ideas to the [ACR subforum](https://aka.ms/ACTInbox/).

## The procedure

1. Identify the opportunity for posting the idea
2. State of the art
3. List of recommendations
4. Communicate recommendations

### Identify the opportunity for posting the idea

Whenever there is a need for a feature which doesn't exist on ACR, it is an opportunity to post in the forum. Note: the forum is not intended to solve issues — that is the scope of a Service Request.

### State of the art

Verify if the idea was posted before submitting. If it exists, take note of its status and follow up accordingly.

### List of recommendations

The following recommendations help create a post that stands out in the forum:

- Describes thoroughly a technical limitation or innovation on existing products/tools/services
- References similar posts but addresses an original concept
- Frames the best technical scenario with text diagrams where applicable (image uploads are unsupported)
- Includes references to similar features in other products/services
- Searches the technical idea on external Microsoft channels: GitHub repos, [ACR public roadmap](https://github.com/orgs/Azure/projects/259), etc.
- Invites readers to understand the business impact
- Indicates desired implementation timeline
- Estimates scope (ACR-only or cross-service)
- Uses Microsoft Copilot to generate additional content

### Communicate recommendations

Share all or a portion of the recommendations with the customer. Note they are recommendations — customers may develop their own approach.

## Conclusion

This procedure guides defining and communicating a high-quality post on the [ACR subforum](https://aka.ms/ACTInbox/).

As a support engineer, you are also invited to post ideas in the forum and spread awareness via email, Teams, Yammer, etc.
