## Purpose
Guidance for engineers and TAs when you're **unexpectedly out of office** (sick, family emergency, urgent personal matters) to enable quick coverage and minimize customer impact.

## TL;DR Summary
1. **Notify**: Email your **manager** and **TA**; CC **ATZMDOBackups@microsoft.com**. Include dates away, expected return, active cases, and coverage needs.
2. **Log time**: Record the absence in **MSVacation** and **MSCaT** when practical.
3. **Flag action items**: Call out Sev 1/A/sensitive cases, customer meetings, or time-sensitive commitments that need attention.

**Note:** Health and family emergencies come first.

## Scope
Use this guidance when the absence is:
- Same-day or short notice
- Unplanned or urgent
- You can't actively manage cases or responsibilities

## Procedure

### 1) Notify (if you can)
Send an email as soon as possible.

**To:** your **manager** and **TA**  
**CC:** **ATZMDOBackups@microsoft.com**

**Subject**  
Unexpected OOF - \<Your Full Name\>  \<Date: YYYY-MM-DD\>

**Body**  
Hi everyone,

- OOF: \<date(s)\>
- Expected return: \<date\> (estimate if unknown)
- Cases needing attention: Case ######  \<status + next step\> (repeat as needed) or No active cases
- Coverage needed: \<customer calls/reviews/escalations/etc.\>

**Guidelines:** Keep it short and actionable; explicitly note if you have no active cases; flag time-sensitive items (customer meetings, Sev 1/A/sensitive cases, commitments).

### 2) Update leave systems
Log the absence when practical:
- **MSVacation**
- **MSCaT**

If you can't do this immediately, complete it when you return or coordinate with your manager.

### 3) Ensure case and responsibility awareness
Before stepping away (if possible):
- Keep **case notes current** (status + next step)
- Call out:
  - Sev 1/A /sensitive cases
  - Customer meetings scheduled during your absence
  - Any **non-case responsibilities** (triage roles, reviews, mentoring, etc.)

Do what you reasonably can; the team will cover the rest.

## Manager and TA responsibilities
On notification of an unexpected OOF, managers and TAs:
- **Acknowledge** and assess immediate action items
- **Ensure** the engineer is removed from the queue
- **Review active cases** and time-sensitive work
- **Coordinate coverage or reassignment** as needed

## Upon your return
- Reconnect with your manager or TA about coverage during your absence
- Review case updates and reclaim ownership where appropriate

## Questions or help
If you're unable to follow this procedure for any reason:
- Contact your **manager** (or **TA** if manager is not available)
- Share what you can
