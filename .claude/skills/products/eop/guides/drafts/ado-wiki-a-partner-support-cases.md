Partner Support (ASfP / PSfP)
=============================

Purpose
-------

This wiki provides clear, consistent guidance for Partner Support cases across CSS, including ASfP (Advanced Support for Partners) and PSfP (Premier Support for Partners). It is intended for support engineers, Technical Advisors (TAs), managers, and leadership to ensure we remain contract-compliant, partner-centric, and aligned globally.

GoDaddy cases are different, read here: [GoDaddy Special Handling 5 7 705](https://dev.azure.com/ASIM-Security/Messaging%20Protection/_wiki/wikis/Messaging%20Protection%20Wiki/8240/GoDaddy-Special-Handling-5-7-705)

* * *
TL;DR  Partner Support (ASfP / PSfP)
-------------------------------------

*   **Partners own the customer relationship.** CSS supports the partner, not the customer directly.
*   **Partners must be included in all communications and calls.** Troubleshooting cannot occur without the partner present.
*   **Scope early and clearly.** Align with the partner and customer on the issue, plan of action, and required data.
*   **CSS is not a replacement for partner support.** We assist with what the partner cannot resolve, while maintaining customer sentiment.
*   **Communicate transparently and on time,** especially during escalations or delays.
*   **Escalate risks immediately.** Involve the partner and management if concerns arise around behavior, engagement, or ownership.
*   **Use each case as a learning opportunity** to strengthen partner capability and reduce repeat escalations.

* * *

What is a Partner Support case?
-------------------------------

Partner Support cases are engagements where a Microsoft partner is the primary support entity for the end customer, and Microsoft CSS is engaged only when the partner requires assistance beyond their scope.

**Key characteristics:**

*   The partner owns the customer relationship and manages expectations.
*   The partner performs initial troubleshooting and triage, Microsoft is generally only responsible for what the Partner cannot fix.
*   CSS supports the partner, not the partners customer directly without the partner present.
*   More info: [Report problems on behalf of a customer - Partner Center | Microsoft Learn](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/partner-center/customers/report-problems-on-behalf-of-a-customer)

* * *

Core Principles
---------------

### 1. Partner-Centric Engagement

*   All conversations, decisions, and communications must occur with the partner.
*   Partners must be included in all emails, meetings, and calls.
*   Troubleshooting calls cannot take place without the partner present.

Microsoft engineers **may work directly** with the end customer when appropriate only if the partner remains actively engaged and included.

### 2. Early and Clear Scoping

During case creation and early engagement:

*   Clearly articulate the scope of the issue for both the partner and customer.
*   Define the plan of action.
*   Explain why specific steps or data are being requested.

### 3. Defined Roles and Responsibilities

Partner responsibilities:
*   Perform initial troubleshooting.
*   Act as the primary interface with the end customer, or is present for communications.
*   Enable the customer to succeed independently where possible.

CSS responsibilities:
*   Resolution of the issue.
*   Assist with issues the partner cannot resolve.
*   Provide expert guidance, validation, and escalation support.

Shared responsibilities:
*   Manage end customer expectations, as long as end customer is being reasonable.

CSS is not a replacement for partner support. Do not withhold answers or sacrifice customer sentiment to enforce this.

### 4. Communication and Transparency

*   Provide timely and meaningful updates, especially for escalations or delays.
*   Adhere to agreed callback times.
*   Communicate in advance if the time cannot be met.

### 5. Escalation and Risk Management

If concerns arise regarding customer control, partner engagement, scope creep, or end customer listed as primary:

*   Immediately notify the partner, CSAM and/or your management.
*   Do not engage with end customer 1:1 without partner.
*   Contact the **Partner CSAM** found in the case contacts list.
*   Support engineers may contact the Partner CSAM directly and keep management aware.

### Partner Engagement as a Learning Opportunity

*   Help partners strengthen their technical depth.
*   Enable partners to better support their customers in the future.
*   Reduce repeat escalations over time.
