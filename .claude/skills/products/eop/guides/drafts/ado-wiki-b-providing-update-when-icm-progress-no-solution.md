# Overview
The below are example emails that we can send which elaborate on how cases are progressing internally. We are often limited in what we can share, however, we can still provide _metadata_ about what is happening internally. We should not be providing emails that are one liners when they're pending PG, we always need to sound empathetic, and ally with the Cx in each email.

Please use these as inspiration only, and ensure that **you're always telling the truth (don't use the wrong template or example)**. These are alternatives to no update currently emails when cases are pending with the Product Group. Copilot can also help you write these emails as well, using the prompt below:

# Best Practices  
- **Set clear expectations:** Avoid promising updates or resolution timelines unless you're confident you can follow through.  
- **End with a personal commitment:** Reassure the customer that you're advocating for them and actively moving things forward.  
- **Provide a follow-up timeline:** Let them know when they can expect to hear from you next.

# Other examples for manually creating updates:
## Case Transferred to Another PG Team / Another PG Team Collaborating on the request
- "The product group has brought in another team to help investigate further, as it looks like this needs deeper guidance."
- "There's been movement behind the scenes. Another engineering team has been looped in to explore other angles, as this is turning out to be a bit more complex than I initially thought."
- "The case has shifted internally to a specialized group, and they're actively investigating now. It looks like this specific ask requires input from multiple teams."
 
## New PG Engineer Assigned
- "A Principal [x] engineer has joined the investigation and is working closely with the team. There's daily discussion happening, but no breakthrough just yet. I'm continuing to track and push for updates."
- "While we're still working toward a solution, a [x] engineer has been looped in to help drive progress. The team is collaborating actively."
- "The team has escalated the case to a Principal engineer. I'll keep you posted once there's something concrete to share, and I'm continuing to follow up on your behalf."
 
## PG Acknowledged but No ETA (First Follow-Up)
- "The Product Group has acknowledged the issue and is reviewing it. I'm keeping a close eye and pushing for updates. If things stall and I'm not able to gain progress, I'll loop in my TA or manager to help escalate."
- "The engineering team is actively reviewing the case. I'll share any updates as soon as they're available."
- "I've raised this case with our product group and they're aware of the impact. There's no timeline yet, but the case is still under review. I'm continuing to follow up."

## Waiting on Internal Dependencies  
- "I wanted to let you know that we're currently waiting on some internal pieces to move forward. I unfortunately can't share more details, but I do want to assure you that there's progress. The case is still active and I'm tracking it closely."  
- "The investigation is progressing through internal channels. I'll keep you informed when we have actionable updates."

## Customer Advocacy Without Update  
- "I've been advocating for your case internally and making sure it stays visible to the right teams. While there's no new update, it's being prioritized."  
- "I'm continuing to push for movement and visibility on your behalf. Thanks so much for your patience."  
- "I'm tracking your case closely with PG, and I'm keeping internal pressure on to make sure it's not overlooked. Please note that while I'm only following up with you bi-daily, I'm asking for updates much more frequently internally."
