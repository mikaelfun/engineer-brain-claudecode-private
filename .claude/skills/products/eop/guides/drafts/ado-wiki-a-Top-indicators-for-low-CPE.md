# Top Indicators for Low CPE (Customer Perceived Effectiveness)

The following are the top indicators that lead to low CPE results **based on real customer verbatims**:

## 1. Case Resolution Time

Customer verbatims:
- *"No resolution, device was reimaged instead. Days to a week between contact from the support engineer... not hear anything back for a week. Rinse, repeat."*
- *"Issue is not yet resolved, no communication for 1 month..."*
- *"Had the ticket open for 1 yr and 5 months and the solution got resolved."*
- *"This has been ongoing for over 6 months and we still don\u2019t have a fix. The call has now been closed and we still haven\u2019t resolved the issue."*
- *"This issue dragged on for 2.5 months. It was passed between 3+ support technicians, none of whom was able to provide support..."*
- *"I got the official response 3 weeks after creating the case. When it was not relevant."*
- *"MS Support took too long."*
- *"It took over 6 months for this to be escalated to the right person."*

## 2. Communication Issues

Customer verbatims relate to engineers who:
- Did not respond timely
- Did not communicate proactively
- Did not explain troubleshooting rationale clearly
- Did not manage customer expectations

## 3. Knowledge / Technical Accuracy

Customer verbatims relate to:
- Engineers providing inaccurate guidance
- Troubleshooting in the wrong direction
- Not identifying root cause
- Providing generic action plans without technical justification

## 4. Case Progression / Process Issues

- Multiple engineers engaged from multiple teams
- Ineffective handoffs between teams
- Case transferred to another team without proper context
- SLA delays at assignment or re-assignment

## Key Takeaways for Engineers

- **Respond promptly** and maintain communication cadence even when no update is available
- **Set clear expectations** about timeline and next steps
- **Understand the full scenario** before starting troubleshooting
- **Provide technical justifications** for your action plans
- **Ensure thorough handoffs** when transferring cases
